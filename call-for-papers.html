CALL FOR PAPERS

TRACKS

All honorable authors are kindly encouraged to contribute to and help shape the conference through submissions of their research abstracts, papers and e-posters. Also, high quality research contributions describing original and unpublished results of conceptual, constructive, empirical, experimental, or theoretical work in all areas of Cognitive Science and Image Processing are cordially invited for presentation at the conference. The conference solicits contributions of abstracts, papers and e-posters that address themes and topics of the conference, including figures, tables and references of novel research materials. Only submit the softcopy of full-length paper.

01

Track 1: Sustainable Materials and Manufacturing

  • Development of environmentally friendly materials, less toxic chemicals, and processes that minimize waste and energy consumption
  • Research into sustainable materials for energy applications (e.g., perovskite solar cells, advanced polymers for batteries)
  • Design of efficient and scalable manufacturing processes for sustainable products, including 3D printing, sustainable supply chains, and waste minimization
02

Track 2: Renewable and Clean Energy Technologies

  • Focus on new catalysts for efficient energy production (e.g., hydrogen production, fuel cells) and energy storage solutions
  • Theoretical and experimental studies on improving energy conversion efficiency (e.g., in solar cells, wind turbines)
  • Implementation of renewable energy systems and infrastructure, such as energy grids, smart grids, and storage solutions
  • Advancements in energy storage technologies (e.g., lithium-ion, solid-state, and flow batteries) to improve efficiency, capacity, and sustainability
03

Track 3: Nanotechnology and Advanced Technologies for Sustainability

  • Development of nanomaterials for energy applications (e.g., enhanced solar cells, energy-efficient lighting, catalysts)
  • Exploring quantum technologies to enhance energy production, storage, and environmental sustainability (e.g., quantum computing for material design or quantum sensors for environmental monitoring)
  • Advances in light-based technologies for clean energy applications, such as advanced photovoltaic devices, LED lighting, and energy-efficient displays
04

Track 4: Climate Change Mitigation and Carbon Capture

  • CO2 capture, conversion, and storage technologies, such as carbon capture and utilization (CCU) methods
  • Understanding and optimizing processes like direct air capture, geo-engineering, and emissions reduction techniques
  • Development of large-scale carbon capture infrastructure, integrating renewable energy with carbon-neutral technologies, and designing carbon-neutral cities
05

Track 5: Environmental Monitoring and Data Analytics

  • Sensors for detecting pollutants, toxins, and greenhouse gases in the environment
  • Development of new sensing technologies using optics, acoustics, or electromagnetic waves to monitor environmental health
  • Integration of data analytics, IoT, and AI to monitor environmental changes in real-time and improve sustainability practices

All the accepted and presented papers will be published in the Scopus/WOS indexed journel.
